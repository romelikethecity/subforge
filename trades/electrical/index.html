Electrical Contractor Data

Verified contacts for commercial electrical contractors, low-voltage specialists, and datacom firms. Service capacity, license class, and bonding data included.

What's included

SubForge's electrical dataset covers actively licensed commercial contractors in this trade across all 50 states. Each record includes the firm name, owner or principal contact, verified email, direct phone, business address, state license number with active status, license class, trade classification, and project type focus where the data supports it.

Data is sourced from state contractor licensing boards and cross-referenced against project filings, business registrations, and firm websites. Every contact is deliverability-tested before inclusion in your list.

Who uses this data

Teams selling construction software, building materials, equipment, equipment rental, surety bonds, finance, and trade association memberships to electrical contractors. If your product or service serves this segment of commercial construction, SubForge provides the contacts you need to reach decision-makers directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What electrical contractor data fields are included?

Every record contains: contractor name, owner contact, verified email, direct phone, business address, license number, license class, bonding capacity tier, and trade specialization. If you need additional fields like project portfolio or union status, let us know when you place your order.

How is the data verified?

We cross-check every contractor against their state board for active license status, validate emails through deliverability testing, verify phone numbers against carrier databases, and confirm firm details against multiple public sources.

How fresh is the data?

Each list is built to order using the most current license records available at the time. We don't recycle old data. Email and phone verification happens in the final 48 hours before delivery, so contact accuracy reflects current status.

Do you cover electrical contractors in all states?

We cover every US jurisdiction. Each state contractor board publishes records on its own schedule. We maintain pipelines for all 50 states and adjust our sourcing to match each board's data refresh cycle.

Can you separate commercial from residential firms?

Every contractor in our database is classified by commercial vs residential focus. We use license class, project records, and firm characteristics to make this distinction. You won't get residential remodelers when you're targeting commercial GCs.
